About This Tracker

This tracker simulates the material costs of the June 21, 2025 U.S. airstrikes on Iran, starting at 00:00 UTC. It focuses on direct hardware costs only: no personnel, logistics, or indirect expenses.

Confirmed Weapons and Aircraft

  • 14 × GBU-57 bunker-busters (~$3.5 million each) → $49 million
  • 24 × Tomahawk missiles (~$2 million each) → $48 million
  • B-2 bomber flight time (~7 bombers × 18 hrs × $135,000/hr) → $17.01 million
  • KC-135 aerial refueling (~40 hrs × $19,900/hr) → $0.8 million

Other Likely Munitions (Conservative Estimate)

  • 40 × JDAM guided bombs (~$25,000 each) → $1 million
  • 4 × AGM-158 JASSM missiles (~$1.5 million each) → $6 million
  • 20 × Small Diameter Bombs (~$40,000 each) → $0.8 million
  • Electronic warfare drones/systems (2 units) → $4 million
  • Defensive interceptor missiles (2 units) → $2 million

Total estimated initial material cost: ~$128.6 million

Operating Costs (Used for Ongoing Per-Second Rate)

For the live running counter, only daily operating costs and ordnance likely to be consumed regularly are counted, excluding expensive one-time bunker-buster bombs and Tomahawk missiles. These include:

  • B-2 bomber flight hours
  • KC-135 aerial refueling
  • Smaller precision weapons like JDAMs, SDBs, and JASSMs
  • Electronic warfare and defensive missile systems

This yields an estimated ongoing cost of about $31.61 million per day, which converts to roughly $366 per second.
